Course Description

This course provides a systematic introduction to the core concepts and cutting-edge developments in artificial intelligence, covering a comprehensive range from foundations to applications. It begins with the basic framework of AI—data, models, and tasks—followed by regression, classification, and the underlying principles of optimization. The course highlights convolutional neural networks and object detection in computer vision, as well as recurrent neural networks, Transformers, and large language models in natural language processing. Generative models, including variational autoencoders, generative adversarial networks, and diffusion models, are also introduced. Students will further explore few-shot learning and multimodal learning to understand their potential in real-world scenarios. The human-computer interaction component covers speech recognition and speech synthesis, demonstrating AI's practical value in interactive technologies. In addition, the course introduces AI security, cultivating awareness of potential risks and critical thinking skills. Balancing theory with practice, this course serves as a systematic entry point and exploration platform for artificial intelligence.

Course Guidelines & Policies

Collaboration Policy:
  • We encourage collaboration between students and studying materials in groups when the purpose of this is to facilitate learning, not to circumvent problems. It is allowed to seek help from other students in understanding the material needed to solve a particular problem.
  • However, students must submit individual material and solutions, unless otherwise specified. Students should declare any collaboration on the first page of homework assignments (or equivalently on exercises).
  • If the instructors believe the collaboration is improper, your grade may be affected.
AI Tools & ChatGPT Usage Policy:
  • We encourage you to use ChatGPT to solve the homework assignment problems (because likely in the future everyone will use it for coding).
  • However, you will need to declare using ChatGPT/LLMs in the homework write-up, as well as the prompts you use.
  • Direct submission of AI-generated code without understanding is prohibited.
  • Note that AI tools should supplement, not replace, learning and problem-solving skills.
Communication & Participation:
  • Regular attendance and participation are expected.
  • Questions and discussions are encouraged during class.
  • Email responses from TAs within 24 hours on weekdays.
